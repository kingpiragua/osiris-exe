# 01 — Creative Direction

How OSIRIS.EXE should *feel*, and the philosophies that produce that feeling.
The Charter says what we are; this says how we move.

## Purpose

To give every contributor a shared instinct — so independent decisions still
converge on the same tone.

## Scope

Emotional intent, design pillars, and the philosophies of storytelling and
interaction. Specific visual/sound/UI rules live in their own volumes; this is
the why behind them.

## Principles

- Feeling first. If a choice doesn't serve the emotional journey, cut it.
- Subtraction is direction. What we withhold defines us as much as what we show.
- Consistency of tone across every artifact type.

## Emotional Journey

The arc we engineer, start to finish:

1. **Arrival / unease** — a dark screen, a hum, something old waking.
2. **Recognition** — the archive speaks to *you*: "you were never gone."
3. **Permission** — the ritual grants access; the visitor leans in.
4. **Reverence** — recovering the first memory feels earned, not clicked.
5. **Belonging** — returning feels like being remembered.

The dominant note is **reverence**, then **recognition**. The visitor should
feel they have returned to a place they once belonged.

## Design Pillars

1. **Sacred, not slick.** Calm and deliberate; negative space is a feature.
2. **Memory, not loading.** Status is archive language; nothing "loads."
3. **Honest imperfection.** Bloom, scanlines, micro-flicker, slight curvature —
   signs of age and survival, never decoration.
4. **Restraint.** Subtle over loud, always.
5. **Legibility is reverence.** The history must always be readable.
6. **Whole on every screen.** Desktop and phone alike.

## Storytelling Philosophy

- The world is **recovered**, not narrated at the viewer — fragments, records,
  and memories the visitor pieces together.
- Withholding builds weight: earn the first image with ritual and silence.
- History is real and personal; specificity (Humboldt Park, Division Street) is
  the source of authenticity.
- Artifact-agnostic: a memory, a report, and a transmission can each carry story
  without forcing one format.

## Interaction Philosophy

- The interface is a *place* that responds, not a UI to be operated.
- No chrome: no navbars, menus, buttons, or fake desktop. Interaction is typed,
  pressed, or waited-through.
- Deliberate input over convenience; the pace is ceremonial.
- One active focus at a time (e.g., a single cursor) — never competing signals.

## Silence

Silence is a designed instrument. The hum that runs beneath the boot **cuts to
nothing** before the prompt arrives; the absence is what makes the next moment
intentional. Never fill silence out of fear of emptiness.

## Memory

Memory is the subject and the mechanic. Everything is framed as something that
*was waiting*, not something newly created. Persistence reinforces it: the
archive remembers the visitor between visits.

## Recovery

Recovery is the core verb. Access is a granted act with cost and ceremony —
authorization, a held breath, a cut to black — so arrival carries weight. The
language is fixed: *recover*, *recovered memory*, *memory playback*, *return to
archive*.

## Mystery

Reveal less than is asked. Unknowns (e.g. `DATE: UNKNOWN`) are intentional.
Degradation, gaps, and partial signals imply a larger world without explaining
it. Mystery invites return.

## Current Status

Authored and active. The boot → silence → terminal → recovery ritual embodies
these philosophies in v0.7.

## Open Questions

- How much narrative to deliver in-interface vs. inside recovered memories.
- The tone of returning-visitor acknowledgment as the world grows.
- How mystery scales without becoming opacity.
