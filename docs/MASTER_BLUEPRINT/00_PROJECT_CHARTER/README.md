# 00 — Project Charter

The constitution of OSIRIS.EXE. Every other volume serves what is written here.
When in doubt, this document wins.

## Purpose

To fix, permanently, what OSIRIS.EXE *is*, what it refuses to be, and how we
will know it is succeeding — so the work can grow for years without losing its
center.

## Scope

Mission, vision, principles, boundaries, and success metrics for the whole
franchise. Not implementation; not lore. Those live in their own volumes.

## Principles

- The Charter is short, absolute, and rarely changed.
- A change here is a deliberate event, dated and explained.
- If a decision elsewhere contradicts the Charter, the decision is wrong.

## Mission

Let people **recover memory** that the world tried to erase — Puerto Rican
diaspora history, lineage, and resistance — through an interface that treats that
act as sacred rather than transactional.

## Vision

OSIRIS.EXE becomes a recognizable *place*: an archive-operating-system with its
own atmosphere, signature, and ritual, where each recovered memory carries weight
because the visitor was guided into it. A universe expressed across many artifact
types, anchored by the recovered memory (motion comic) as its flagship form.

## Core Principles

1. **Recovery, not consumption.** The visitor is granted access to memory; they
   never "open," "view," or "browse."
2. **Reverence over slickness.** Calm, deliberate, sacred. Silence is allowed.
3. **The medium is the message.** A record that refuses deletion, on hardware
   that remembers — the CRT and the DISK are meaning, not decoration.
4. **Restraint.** One quiet, intentional moment beats constant motion.
5. **Truth held with care.** Real history (Humboldt Park, the diaspora) is
   treated with accuracy and respect, never as set-dressing.
6. **Legibility is reverence.** Effects never fight the content.

## Creative Goals

- Make accessing the first memory feel like being *granted permission*.
- Build an interface so distinct it is recognizable from a single frame.
- Establish a ritual grammar (boot → silence → terminal → recover) reusable for
  every future artifact.
- Earn emotional weight before showing imagery.

## What OSIRIS.EXE Is

- An archive operating system for a fictional universe.
- A sacred, green-phosphor CRT experience.
- A home for many artifact types (recovered memories, reports, transmissions,
  audio, images), unified by ritual and tone.
- A personal, decolonial act of remembering, authored by Disk Darián.

## What OSIRIS.EXE Is NOT

- ❌ A website, dashboard, or app with menus, navbars, buttons, or a fake desktop.
- ❌ A hacker terminal, Matrix homage, or "I'm in" aesthetic.
- ❌ Loud, flashy, or fast. No heavy glitch for spectacle.
- ❌ A content feed to be scrolled or binged.
- ❌ Generic system/OS theater (BIOS, loading bars, Windows references).

## Long-term Vision

A multi-artifact archive with many recovered memories and supporting records,
each reachable through the same ritual; persistence that makes returning feel
like being remembered; and an expandable engine that lets new memories be
authored without forcing every piece of content into one format.

## Development Philosophy

- Every feature must deepen the feeling of recovering memory.
- Reuse before rebuild; keep the system component-based and expandable.
- Ship deliberately; verify before claiming done.
- Document decisions here and in the relevant volume — one source of truth.

## Success Metrics

- **Emotional:** first-time visitors describe the experience as *moving*,
  *eerie*, or *sacred* — not "cool" or "a website."
- **Recognition:** the interface (CRT + DISK) is identifiable at a glance.
- **Engagement quality:** visitors complete the ritual rather than skipping.
- **Expandability:** a new recovered memory can be added without redesigning the
  system.
- **Integrity:** no shipped element violates the "What It Is NOT" list.

## Current Status

Authored and active (v0.7). Success metrics are qualitative for now; measurement
approach is an open question.

## Open Questions

- How (and whether) to measure the emotional metrics without breaking tone.
- Canonical relationship between `osiris-exe`, `osiris-motion-comic`, and any
  future distribution surfaces.
- Monetization / sustainability stance, if any.
